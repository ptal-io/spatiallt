Spatial Lightning Talks @ McGill

To cultivate an interdisciplinary community of spatial thinkers, the Platial Analysis Lab & Geographic Information Centre are happy to host their inaugural Spatial@McGill Lightning Talks event. This is an engaging, fast-paced gathering where faculty, students, staff, and community members share their spatially-related research and ideas in concise, three-minute presentations.

Enlighten us, but make it quick!

Designed to spark conversation and collaboration, these rapid-fire talks highlight the central role of spatial reasoning and representation across disciplines. We invite participation from any discipline... from geography, environmental science, and urban planning to health, information technology, and humanities. Each spatial speaker has just three minutes to present their work, offering a snapshot of the diverse ways spatial thinking shapes research and real-world applications.

Join us for this inaugural event, connect with other spatial thinkers, and explore the many ways spatial perspectives enrich scholarship and practice at McGill and beyond. Registration is free and pizza lunch will be provided.
